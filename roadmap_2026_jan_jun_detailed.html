Nedits Edition — Detailed Skills Roadmap (Jan–Jun 2026)

Six printable pages: each page = one month. Open this page in Chrome (or any browser) and use Print → Save as PDF with A4 paper. Each month contains week-by-week tasks, resources, daily routine, tools and deliverables.

January

2026 — Photo Editing I

Focus: Raster fundamentals — Layers, Selections, Masks, Basic Retouch

Goal: Become confident with Photoshop/GIMP workspace and perform non-destructive edits. Deliver 10 before/after images and 1 composite.

Week 1 — Setup & Basics (Days 1–7)

  • Install & configure: Photoshop (preferred) or GIMP; set up workspace, panels (Layers, Adjustments, History).
  • Core tools: Move, Marquee, Lasso, Quick Selection, Crop, Transform.
  • File formats: PSD (working), PNG (web), JPG (compressed), TIFF/PDF (print).
  • Daily practice: 45–60 min — open 5 images and apply crop, transform, and simple layer adjustments.
Tools: Photoshop / GIMP / Lightroom (optional)

Week 2 — Selections & Masks (Days 8–14)

  • Master Quick Selection, Magic Wand, Lasso, and Pen Tool basics; practice edge refining and Select & Mask workspace.
  • Learn layer masks vs erasing (non-destructive approach).
  • Practice: Remove background from 20 different photos including hair/complex edges.
  • Tip: Save selections as channels for complex composites.

Week 3 — Adjustments & Blending (Days 15–21)

  • Adjustment layers: Levels, Curves, Hue/Saturation, Color Balance, Vibrance.
  • Blend modes: Multiply, Overlay, Screen — learn use-cases.
  • Practice: Create 5 mood-graded images (moody, warm, clean product, vintage, cinematic).
  • Deliverable: 5 color-graded images + style note (which adjustments used).

Week 4 — Retouch & Non‑Destructive Workflow (Days 22–31)

  • Retouch tools: Clone Stamp, Healing Brush, Spot Healing; dodge & burn basics.
  • Smart Objects & filters; learn to use Camera Raw filter non-destructively.
  • Batch basics: Actions (record/ playback) & Image Processor for exports.
  • Project: Composite 1 scene (background replacement + color match).

Learning Resources & Where to Study

  • Free: YouTube — PiXimperfect, Phlearn, Photoshop Training Channel (search these names).
  • Free: GIMP tutorials (if using GIMP) + official Adobe beginner docs.
  • Optional paid: Udemy Photoshop complete course or Adobe official pathway.
Daily 45–90 min practice depending on CA schedule.
Deliverables 10 before/after images, 1 composite, Actions file (resize/export).
Checklist Install software • Create project folder • Save PSD versions • Export web copies.
Monthly focus: get comfortable with workflow & non-destructive edits.

February

2026 — Photo Editing II

Focus: Portrait & Product Retouching, Lightroom workflow, Batch Processing

Goal: Learn portrait retouching techniques and produce consistent product images. Deliver 3 themed sets and 1 batch export workflow.

Week 1 — Lightroom Basics & Catalogs

  • Understand Lightroom import, catalog structure, Develop panel, crop, lens correction.
  • Practice: Import a photoshoot and create basic edits for 20 photos.
  • Learn export presets and create 2 export profiles (web & print).

Week 2 — Portrait Retouching Advanced

  • Frequency separation (skin texture vs colour) basics, dodge & burn for shaping, eye/teeth whitening, hair touch-ups.
  • Practice: Retouch 6 portraits — keep natural look, avoid over-smoothing.
  • Deliverable: 6 retouched portraits + step notes (what was done).

Week 3 — Batch Processing & Actions

  • Photoshop Actions: record repeatable steps for resizing, watermarking, color profile conversion.
  • Lightroom presets: create 3 reusable presets (clean product, warm lifestyle, moody).
  • Practice: Create batch action to export 50 product images with watermark.

Week 4 — E‑commerce Product Edits

  • Clipping path basics, shadow creation, white-background consistency, color correction for product catalogs.
  • Project: Edit 10 product images for a local shop (offer to a shop for practice).
  • Deliverable: Product pack (10 web-ready JPEGs) + 2 preview mockups.

Resources & Pro Tips

  • Use Unsplash/Pexels for practice images. Learn Lightroom export best practices.
  • Keep non-destructive workflow: use smart objects and adjustment layers; save actions and presets in a synced folder (Google Drive).
  • Optional: Study "product photography retouch" playlists on YouTube; consider small local paid gigs to practice real-world images.
Daily 45–90 min: mix Lightroom and Photoshop tasks.
Deliverables 3 themed sets (12 images each), 10 product images, 3 LR presets, Actions file.
Monthly focus: consistent edits & workflow automation for volume work.

March

2026 — Graphic Design Principles

Focus: Color, Typography, Layout & Practical Branding

Goal: Master the visual rules that make designs readable and memorable. Deliver a small brand mini-kit and redesign 4 items.

Week 1 — Color Theory & Palettes

  • Learn hue/saturation/value, contrast, complementary and analogous palettes.
  • Create 10 palettes: brand, muted, vibrant, monochrome, pastel.
  • Practice: Apply palettes to a poster and a social post each.

Week 2 — Typography Essentials

  • Hierarchy, font pairing, line-height, kerning, readable body copy vs display fonts.
  • Practice: Design 3 typographic posters focusing solely on type.
  • Tools: Google Fonts, Typewolf for inspiration, try variable fonts.

Week 3 — Grids & Composition

  • Grid systems (8pt, column grids), white space, alignment and visual flow.
  • Practice: Re-layout a cluttered flyer into a clean grid-based layout.
  • Deliverable: 2 refined layouts (poster + brochure page).

Week 4 — Apply to Branding

  • Combine color & typography into a consistent look; design menu, poster, business card & brochure for a cafe project.
  • Document rationale: why colors/fonts chosen, usage rules.
  • Deliverable: Brand mini-kit for the café (logo options, 2 posters, menu, business card).

Where to Learn & Practice

  • Free: Canva design school articles for basics, YouTube design theory videos.
  • Read or watch summaries of "The Non-Designer's Design Book".
  • Practice with real local examples: redesign a shop's flyer or create a menu for a tiffin service.
Daily 40–75 min: theory + practical layout.
Deliverables 10 palettes, 3 typographic posters, 2 layouts, café brand mini-kit.
Monthly focus: build visual grammar that applies across projects.

April

2026 — Vector & Logo Design I

Focus: Pen tool mastery, vector workflows, iconography & logo ideation

Goal: Create clean scalable logos and an icon set. Deliver a 24-icon set & 2 logo concepts vectorized.

Week 1 — Illustrator Setup & Pen Tool Drills

  • Install Illustrator or Inkscape. Learn artboards, pen tool, anchor control, bezier curves basics.
  • Daily drill: trace 10 shapes using pen tool and refine paths.
  • Practice: Recreate simple logos to understand curves and balance.

Week 2 — Icon Design & Pathfinder

  • Learn shape building: pathfinder, compound shapes, strokes vs fills.
  • Practice: Design 12 icons (monoline) for the café brand.
  • Deliverable: 12 SVG icons exported at multiple sizes.

Week 3 — Logo Sketch → Vectorize

  • Sketch 20 quick logo concepts on paper; shortlist 4; vectorize 2 in software.
  • Iterate variations: stacked, horizontal, mark-only.
  • Practice export: SVG (web), EPS/PDF (print), PNG @2x.

Week 4 — Responsive Logos & Branding Assets

  • Create logo guidelines for spacing, color usage, and minimal reproducible mark.
  • Produce favicon & app icon variants; test legibility at 16–32 px.
  • Deliverable: 2 logo concepts + 24-icon pack + export zip.

Resources & Practice Tips

  • Use vector grids and preview at small sizes constantly. Save SVG cleanly (no excess metadata).
  • Learn shortcuts (P for pen, A for direct select, Shift for constrained shapes).
  • Offer a few free logo concepts to a local shop for real feedback.
Daily 45–90 min pen + icon drills.
Deliverables 24 icons (SVG), 2 vector logos (SVG/EPS/PNG).
Monthly focus: vector precision & logo scalability.

May

2026 — Branding Basics

Focus: Complete brand system, mockups & guidelines

Goal: Turn logo + visuals into a usable brand identity. Deliver a polished 8–12 page brand guide.

Week 1 — Research & Moodboards

  • Competitor research: collect 12 references; create a moodboard in Figma/Pinboard.
  • Decide voice & tone: modern, rustic, premium, friendly — document choices.
  • Practice: Build 2 alternate moodboards (vibrant vs muted).

Week 2 — Palette & Typography System

  • Choose primary, secondary, accent colors and neutral scale.
  • Choose typefaces: display, heading, body, UI; set scale & line-height.
  • Deliverable: color tokens + typography token sheet.

Week 3 — Visual Language & Photography Style

  • Define photo mood, image treatments (filters/presets) and patterns or textures.
  • Create mockups: packaging, bag, signboard, social post template.
  • Practice: apply filters/presets to sample images for consistent look.

Week 4 — Brand Guideline Draft

  • Assemble 8–12 page PDF: logo usage, spacing, do/don't, color codes, type specs, sample applications.
  • Export both print PDF (CMYK hints) and web PDF (RGB optimized).
  • Deliverable: Brand guideline PDF ready to share with clients.

Where to Get Assets & Mockups

  • Free mockups: search for PSD/PNG mockups; use Figma mockup kits for quick previews.
  • Organize tokens into a reusable Figma library for future projects.
  • Tip: Keep brand files layered & well-named for handoff to clients.
Daily 45–75 min research + design time.
Deliverables 8–12 page brand guide PDF, token pack, 3 mockups.
Monthly focus: produce a client-ready brand package.

June

2026 — Portfolio Sprint I

Focus: Case-studies, presentable portfolio & Behance/Dribbble launch

Goal: Convert projects from Jan–May into 2 polished case-studies and a 6–8 page printable portfolio.

Week 1 — Case Study Structure

  • Write narrative: problem → research → sketches → iterations → final solution → results.
  • Choose 2 flagship projects (e.g., Café brand & Milk Delivery prototype).
  • Practice: Draft 1-page storyboards for each project.

Week 2 — Visuals & Mockups

  • Prepare high-res images: hero shots, process screenshots, close-ups.
  • Create presentation layouts in Figma optimized for A4 print and web hero shots.
  • Deliverable: printable layout for each case study (3 pages each).

Week 3 — Publish Preparation

  • Behance/Dribbble shots: crop sizes, composition, write concise captions and process bullets.
  • Create thumbnail images for each case study; write SEO-friendly titles and tags.
  • Practice: Post one teaser on Instagram to gather feedback.

Week 4 — Portfolio Export & Print Check

  • Export a 6–8 page PDF portfolio: ensure bleed / safe margins for print.
  • Proofread copy and run a print test on A4 — check colors and layout.
  • Deliverable: Final portfolio PDF + Behance live post + Instagram teaser content.

Checklist & Next Steps

  • Get two local clients (can be exchange-for-work) for real testimonials.
  • Set up a simple portfolio site (Next.js/Netlify) in future months — link portfolio PDF.
  • Keep a folder: /2026-Jun-Portfolio with all source files & exports compressed.
Daily 45–90 min polishing and packaging.
Deliverables 2 case studies live + 6–8 page printable portfolio PDF.
Monthly focus: packaging your work for clients—print & online.
